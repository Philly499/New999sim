🚨 When should you call 999?

🚑

Ambulance

Someone is seriously injured, unconscious, or having a medical emergency

🚒

Fire Brigade

There is a fire, or someone is trapped in a dangerous situation

🚔

Police

A crime is happening, someone is in danger, or you feel unsafe

⚠️ Not an emergency? Use 111 for medical advice, or 101 for non-urgent police matters. Calling 999 when it's not needed can slow down help for people who really need it.

📋 What happens when you call 999?

1

You'll hear: "Emergency, which service do you require?"

Say "Police", "Fire", or "Ambulance" clearly.

2

They'll ask where you are

Try to give your full address, postcode, or describe nearby landmarks.

3

Explain what is happening

Stay calm and answer their questions clearly and honestly.

4

Stay on the line

Don't hang up unless told to. The operator may give you important instructions while help is on the way.

💡 Did you know?

  • You can call 999 even if you have no signal — your phone will try every network available.
  • If you can't speak, stay on the line and press 55 when prompted — this alerts police.
  • You can text 999 if you are deaf, hard of hearing, or cannot speak — but you must register first at emergencysms.net
  • 999 calls are free — even with no credit or from a locked phone.
  • The average response time for a life-threatening 999 call is around 7 minutes.
